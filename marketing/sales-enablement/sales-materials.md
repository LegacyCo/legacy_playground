## 1. BRAND STRATEGY CALL FRAMEWORK

### Pre-Call Research Template

Before every strategy call, complete this 10-minute research brief:

**Company Research:**
- [ ] Visit their website — note homepage headline, key messaging, brand voice
- [ ] Review their social profiles (last 10 posts) — note consistency, tone, content type
- [ ] Check their LinkedIn page — note how they describe themselves
- [ ] Google "[Company Name] reviews" — check G2, Clutch, Google reviews if applicable
- [ ] Note any obvious brand inconsistencies or opportunities

**Conversation Starters Prepared Based On:**
- Most obvious brand gap identified in research
- Industry context (what are competitors doing?)
- Company growth stage (startup/scaling/established?)

---

### Strategy Call Script & Framework

**Call Duration:** 30 minutes (hard stop, respects their time)

**PHASE 1 — OPEN (3–5 minutes)**

> "Thanks for making time today. Before we dive in, I want to be upfront: this call has no agenda except to give you real value. If we're a fit to work together, great. If not, you'll still leave with clarity on where your brand stands. Fair?"

*[Confirm they're in a good place to talk, not distracted]*

> "To make the most of our time — can you give me a 60-second snapshot of your business? What you do, who you serve, and where you are right now?"

---

**PHASE 2 — DIAGNOSE (12–15 minutes)**

Listen actively. Let them talk. Ask:

**Brand Clarity Questions:**
- "If I asked five of your best clients to describe what makes you different from competitors — what would they say?"
- "When someone asks what you do at a networking event, what do you say? And does it feel accurate?"
- "What's your brand's single biggest strength in the market right now? And what's the biggest gap?"

**Pain Amplification Questions:**
- "Are you happy with the quality of leads/clients you're attracting right now?"
- "When you lose a deal, what's usually the reason you hear?"
- "Do your clients refer you? If so, how often — and is it unprompted or do you have to ask?"

**Growth Ambition Questions:**
- "Where do you want the brand to be in 2–3 years?"
- "What would have to be true about your brand for you to hit that?"
- "What's the biggest thing standing between where you are and that vision?"

**Internal Signals:**
- "Can your team articulate what makes you different in one sentence? Consistently?"
- "Do you have a documented brand strategy? Or is it mostly in your head?"

---

**PHASE 3 — INSIGHT DELIVERY (8–10 minutes)**

After diagnosis, deliver 2–3 specific, honest observations:

> "Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm seeing..."

**Insight delivery formula:**
- Observation: What you noticed (specific, not generic)
- Implication: Why it matters / what it's costing them
- Direction: What the solution category looks like

**Example Insight Block:**
> "What I'm noticing is that when you describe what you do, you're leading with process — which is natural when you're close to your work. But your ideal clients are buying the outcome, not the process. And when I looked at your website before this call, I noticed your headline describes the service, not the transformation.
>
> The implication of that gap is that people coming to your site are trying to figure out if you're right for them — and they're having to work for that answer. Strong brands make that obvious in 5 seconds.
>
> The direction here is a messaging architecture rebuild — starting with your positioning statement and working down to every customer-facing touchpoint. That's work we do in our Brand Foundation Engagement."

---

**PHASE 4 — TRANSITION & NEXT STEP (3–5 minutes)**

> "So, given what we've talked about — what feels most urgent to address?"

*[Let them answer. Then:]*

> "Here's what I'd recommend as a next step..."

**Option A — Strong fit → Propose engagement:**
> "I think you're a strong fit for our Brand Foundation Engagement. What I'd suggest is [describe next step — proposal, deep-dive call, etc.]. Would it be useful to send over a brief on what that looks like?"

**Option B — Not quite ready → Bridge to lower-commitment offer:**
> "I think you're close, but not quite at the stage where a full brand engagement makes sense yet. What I'd suggest is starting with [program / course / audit service]. That'll give you the foundation to do this right when the time comes."

**Option C — Not a fit → Graceful exit:**
> "Honestly, I don't think Brand Discipleship is the right fit for where you are right now. Here's what I think would actually serve you better: [Genuine recommendation]. I'd rather send you in the right direction than take on work that's not right."

## 3. OBJECTION HANDLING GUIDE

### Objection 1: "We can't afford this right now."

**Acknowledge first:**
> "I hear that — budget timing is always a real consideration."

**Reframe:**
> "Can I ask — what would it mean for your business if in 12 months you were able to raise your prices 20–30% and close more deals without negotiating? That's often what clients experience after this work. The question isn't really whether you can afford it — it's whether you can afford to keep operating with a brand that's costing you that."

**Bridge options:**
> "If the full engagement isn't right for timing, we have a Phase 1-only option that delivers your positioning strategy for [lower investment]. It's a fraction of the full engagement but gives you the most critical output. Want me to send that over?"

---

### Objection 2: "We tried a rebrand before and it didn't work."

**Validate:**
> "That's more common than you'd think. And usually it's because the rebrand was a visual update without a strategic foundation underneath it. Beautiful execution of a vague position still results in a vague brand."

**Differentiate:**
> "The Brand Discipleship approach is different because we refuse to touch identity until the strategy is bulletproof. Phase 1 and 2 are pure strategy. By the time we get to visual identity, we know exactly what we're expressing and why."

---

### Objection 3: "I need to think about it / talk to my partner."

**Respect and clarify:**
> "Of course — this is a significant decision. Can I ask: is there specific information that would make that conversation easier? I'm happy to send additional context, case studies, or schedule a call that includes your partner directly."

**Timeline anchor:**
> "I'll mention that we do limit the number of engagements we take on at a time. The spot I'm holding for [proposed start date] is available through [date]. After that, the next opening would be [timeline]. Just want to make sure you have that context."

---

### Objection 4: "I think we can do this ourselves."

**Respect:**
> "Honestly, you probably can — and if you have the internal capacity and strategic clarity to execute this framework, you should. Some of our best relationships started with someone doing Phase 1 themselves and then coming back for Phase 2 onward."

**Plant the seed:**
> "Where companies typically run into trouble doing this internally is: proximity bias (it's hard to see your own brand clearly), competing priorities (brand strategy gets deprioritized when revenue is urgent), and the missing outside perspective that surfaces your blind spots. If you hit any of those walls, we're here."

---

### Objection 5: "We're not ready yet — we need to [grow more / fix operations / etc.] first."

**Validate:**
> "That makes sense. Brand is most powerful when the business behind it is ready to receive the growth it enables."

**Gentle challenge:**
> "I'll say this: we've found that most businesses that say they need to get ready for brand work actually need brand work in order to get ready. The clarity that comes from the positioning and story process often accelerates everything else — it helps founders make faster decisions, build better teams, and attract better clients even before the full brand system is deployed."

**Offer a bridge:**
> "If you're not ready for a full engagement, starting with our Brand Strategy-only package gets you the positioning foundation now — which will make every business decision between now and your 'ready' point clearer."
