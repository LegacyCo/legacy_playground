# Re-Engagement Email Sequence — Brand Discipleship
**Purpose: Win back inactive subscribers (no open in 60–90+ days)**
**Sequence Length: 4 emails over 14 days, then sunset protocol**

---

## SEQUENCE STRATEGY

Re-engagement sequences serve two purposes:
1. **Win back** subscribers who've gone cold but are still potentially valuable
2. **Clean the list** by identifying truly disengaged subscribers to suppress

The tone should shift from educational to personal, direct, and low-pressure. This is not another nurture sequence. It's a human moment that earns attention through honesty.

---

## POST-SUNSET PROTOCOL

**Subscribers who don't engage with any of the 4 re-engagement emails:**
1. Move to "Suppressed" segment — do not delete, in case they re-subscribe organically
2. Do not send any further marketing emails for 6 months minimum
3. Eligible to be reactivated via paid retargeting (Meta/Google custom audiences) — they've shown interest, they're just email-inactive
4. After 12 months of inactivity, review for permanent removal based on list hygiene policy

**Subscribers who engage with re-engagement emails but don't convert:**
1. Return to standard nurture sequence at the beginning (Email 1)
2. Tag as "Re-engaged" for segment tracking
3. Flag for personalized outreach if score is high (opened 3+ re-engagement emails)
