## 4. TONE OF VOICE

**Primary tone:** Bold, direct, and inspiring — like a visionary mentor who cuts through the noise.

**Do:**
- Use short, punchy sentences with occasional long ones for rhythm
- Make bold declarative statements: "Your brand is leaking revenue." not "You might want to consider..."
- Use movement language: build, ignite, convert, lead, move, believe, grow
- Reference real business outcomes: retention, LTV, CAC, brand equity, pricing power
- Speak to identity and aspiration, not just tactics

**Don't:**
- Use buzzword soup: "synergy," "disruptive," "holistic solutions"
- Be mealy-mouthed or over-qualify every claim
- Write in passive voice
- Over-promise without substance ("10x your business overnight")
- Sound like everyone else

## 9. VISUAL DIRECTION FRAMEWORK

### Color Palette Guidance
- **Primary:** Deep navy (#0D1B2A) — Authority, trust, depth
- **Accent:** Electric gold/amber (#F5A623) — Energy, conviction, warmth
- **Neutral:** Off-white/cream (#FAF7F2) — Clarity, space, approachability
- **Alert/CTA:** Signal red (#E63946) — Urgency, action, passion

### Typography Direction
- **Headlines:** Bold sans-serif (Clash Display, Neue Haas, or similar) — strength, modernity
- **Body:** Clean humanist sans (Inter, Aktiv Grotesk) — readability, warmth
- **Accent/Quote:** Serif or script for testimonials and pull quotes — credibility, humanity

### Imagery Direction
- **Photography:** Real people, real environments — avoid stock clichés (fake handshakes, laptops in cafés)
- **Motion:** Kinetic text, brand reveal moments, behind-the-scenes transformation footage
- **Graphic devices:** Bold typography as design element, bold geometric shapes, strategic use of white space
- **Contrast principle:** Dark/dramatic hero imagery → light/open content sections

### Do/Don't Visual Summary
- DO: Contrast, bold type, confident white space, intentional color pops
- DON'T: Clip art, gradient overload, serif/sans mismatch, inconsistent logo usage
